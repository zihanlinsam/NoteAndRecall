### The Speed of Light Is Limit
- **Tags**: #physics #relativity #light
- **Recalls**: 0
- **Created**: 2026-07-11 11:00 (Unknown)
- **Source**: TEXT

The speed of light in a vacuum is exactly **299,792,458 meters per second** — the universe's speed limit. According to Einstein's special relativity, as an object approaches light speed:
- Time slows down (time dilation)
- Length contracts in the direction of motion
- Mass increases (requiring infinite energy to reach c)

Nothing with mass can reach light speed. Light particles (photons) can travel at c precisely because they have **zero mass**.

### The 80/20 Rule (Pareto Principle)
- **Tags**: #business #productivity #economics
- **Recalls**: 5
- **Created**: 2026-07-15 16:00 (Unknown)
- **Source**: TEXT

The Pareto Principle states that **roughly 80% of effects come from 20% of causes**. Examples:
- 80% of sales come from 20% of customers
- 80% of software bugs come from 20% of code
- 80% of wealth is owned by 20% of the population
- 80% of results come from 20% of effort

Named after Italian economist **Vilfredo Pareto**, who noticed 80% of land in Italy was owned by 20% of the population. The principle is a useful heuristic for **prioritization** — focus on the vital few, not the trivial many.

### Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle
- **Tags**: #quantum #physics #uncertainty
- **Recalls**: 3
- **Created**: 2026-07-18 09:00 (Unknown)
- **Source**: TEXT

Heisenberg's uncertainty principle states that we **cannot simultaneously know both the exact position and exact momentum** of a particle. The more precisely we know one, the less precisely we know the other. This isn't a limitation of measurement — it's a fundamental property of quantum systems. The uncertainty is given by: Δx · Δp ≥ h/(4π), where h is Planck's constant. This principle sets a fundamental limit on what can be known about the universe.

### The Difference Between Weather and Climate

- **Recalls**: 1

- **Source**: TEXT

"Climate is what you expect; weather is what you get." — Robert Heinlein.

Everything we can see — stars, galaxies, planets — makes up only **5% of the universe**.

### The Double-Slit Experiment
- **Tags**: #physics #quantum #experiment
- **Recalls**: 4
- **Created**: 2026-07-22 18:00 (Unknown)
- **Source**: TEXT

The double-slit experiment is one of the most mind-bending experiments in physics. When particles (like electrons) are fired at a barrier with two slits:
- If **unobserved**, they create an **interference pattern** (like waves)
- If **observed/measured**, they behave like **individual particles** (two bands)

### Why Are Flamingos Pink?
- **Tags**: #biology #animals #evolution
- **Recalls**: 0
- **Created**: 2026-07-22 19:00 (Unknown)
- **Source**: TEXT

Flamingos are born **grey or white** — they turn pink because of their diet. They eat algae and brine shrimp that contain **carotenoid pigments** (the same compounds that make carrots orange and tomatoes red). The liver breaks down these pigments into pink and orange molecules that are deposited in the feathers, skin, and beak. Flamingos in zoos that don't get a proper diet will gradually turn white. **Baby flamingos get their color from crop milk** produced by parents, which also contains carotenoids.

### The Internet's Physical Infrastructure
- **Tags**: #technology #internet #infrastructure
- **Recalls**: 2
- **Created**: 2026-07-22 20:00 (Unknown)
- **Source**: TEXT

The internet is often described as a "cloud," but it's physically very real:
- **Underwater cables**: 99% of international data travels through ~400 submarine fiber-optic cables, stretching 1.2 million km across ocean floors
- **Data centers**: Google, Amazon, and Microsoft operate hundreds of massive data centers worldwide, each consuming 100+ MW of electricity
- **Undersea cable repairs**: Specialized ships repair the ~100 cable breaks that occur annually (often from ship anchors or fishing trawlers)
- **Latency is physics**: Data in fiber travels at ~200,000 km/s (2/3 light speed). London to Sydney takes about 150ms one-way — limited by the speed of light, not technology.
